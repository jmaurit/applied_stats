About the course

This course is about how to do a complete data analysis with modern tools and methods. Statistical models, methods and tests are important components of this course, but the course also introduces data-management, transformation, visualisering and descreptiv analysis.

This is a Do Course

The idea behind how this course is organized is that the best way to learn how to do data-analysis is to do data analysis. From the first class period you will be actively working with data. The evaluation in this course will also be on a term project where you will complete all steps of a data analysis. You will need to work actively throughout the semester in order to do well in the course.

Python and co.

In this course we use Python and associated data analysis libraries. This is why:

  • Python (together with R) are the tools that are most used in business and industry. Students that can combine a technical competance with these tools with a solid understanding of business and economics are in demand.
  • Many other course at NTNU as well as other universities in statistics, machine learning and business analytics make use of Python.
  • Python and its associated libraries are open-source, transparent and have a large network of deveopers and users.
  • It is often easy to find help when you have problems in discussion forums like https://stackoverflow.com .
  • As a NTNU Bachelor student, you should already have had an introduction to the basics of using Python.

The course is difficult and useful

Student feedback about the course is that it is one of the most difficult they have taken during their bachelor degree. You will mostly experience some confusion and frustration. This is a normal part of learning something difficult.

Another common feedback is that students learned a lot. You won't learn everything about statistics and data analysis in this course. But you will learn the tools and skills necessary to yourself learn nearly any method or technique.

Course setup

Labs

The course is built around weekly "labs". Each lab consists of a mix of explanation, short videos and code that you should actively replicate. At the end of each lab there are problems. You should work in small groups to work through the labs and then to do the problems at the end of the lab.

Class sessions

We will for the most part not have traditional lectures in this course. Most of the information you need will be in the labs or the text book.

Instead, we use the class times as active work periods and help sessions. Class sessions are where you can work on problems and get help from both me and (if available) a teaching assistant).

To make effective use of this time, you should come to class prepared. You should have skimmed through the readings for that week and looked through the week's lab. It is probably a good idea to start replicating the lab and start on the problems ahead of class period as well.

Assignments and colloquiem groups

In order to get course approval, you will need to turn in 4 assignments over the course of the semester. These problem sets consist of a subset of the weekly problems at the end of each lab. You should work individually or in small groups (2 or 3 people) to complete the labs.

However, you will also organize yourselves into Colloquiem groups

of between 4 and 8 people. The point of the Colloquiem groups is to create a group that you can discuss problems with and get help from. There is only one instructor and potentially more than 100 students, so your main source of help and support will necessarily be other students. For the obligatory assignment, while you should be working in small groups or alone, you will only turn in one assignment

Evaluation and term project

The course culminates in a term project which you can work on individually or together with one other person (no exceptions will be given for more people per group). You should start working on the term project as soon as possible. Details are provided below.

    Om vurderingen

    Det er mye mer læring i å gjøre en prosjektProblem sammenlignet med å sitte for eksamen. Det er derfor vi har prosjektProblem i dette kurset. Vurderingen blir derimot noe diffus. I dette kurset bruker jeg en velkvalifisert ekstern-sensur som vil vurdere Problemne på en rimelig og fornuftig måte. Men det er vanskelig å si noe om akkurat hvor karaktergrensene ligger i forkant. Mitt beste råd: Skriv en Problem som du selv synes er interessante og som du er stolt av. Dette vil vanligvis føre til en grei karakter.

    Jeg kan generelt sett ikke si noe i forkant om hva man må gjøre for å oppnå en viss karakter. Men for å sette riktige forventninger, i følge NTNUs retningslinjer er en A-karakter reservert for eksepsjonelle Problemr. Du kan ha en veldig bra Problem uten at noe nødvendigvis er feil og fortsatt ikke få en A.
